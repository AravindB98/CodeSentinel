A letter of reflection and thanks

Professor Nik Bear Brown

Dear Professor Brown,

I would like to begin by acknowledging, with sincere gratitude, your steady support, motivation, and guidance with AI tools throughout the semester. Your mentorship was instrumental at every stage of this project, from the earliest ideation sessions, through the iterative phases of creation and refinement, and through to its successful completion. Without your encouragement to think ambitiously and your practical guidance on how to use modern generative AI tools responsibly and effectively, the work I am submitting this semester would not have taken the shape it did.

Greetings. As discussed in our last class, I am writing to reflect on this semester’s work, and on how I have learned and grown as a person after taking your INFO 7375 Prompt Engineering and Generative AI during the Spring 2026 semester.

The semester started with watching your videos on YouTube, from What is Prompt Engineering? to the Jane Austen thought experiment to the Personas study. From being just viewers and commenters, we moved to creating videos of our own as part of assignments; that progression from consuming to producing has stayed with me. The assignments themselves turned out to be portfolio pieces. Each one taught us things about AI that would ordinarily take years, compressed into weeks and days, and your motivation for creating portfolio-grade work directly inspired me to build out my own website in that direction.

With your constant encouragement, and by simply listening closely in class, I was able to use AI tools effectively to the point where I feel I have somewhat mastered the art of prompt engineering. I applied the skills learned in class to bring a longstanding idea of mine to a published paper titled Quantum Enhanced Memory Architectures for Graph-based AI Systems (QEMA-G).

Beyond publishing a paper, you kindled an interest in writing in all of us. You encouraged me to contribute a chapter to your forthcoming book on agentic systems. Who knew a single course could lead to all of this? I was also able to contribute to the NortheasternISE Substack, and I started my own Substack, where I have now published more than thirty articles over the course of the semester.

Your teaching also drew me deeper into research and leadership. This semester I helped re-instate AI Skunkworks, our graduate research-focused club, essentially from the ground up, and earned GSG Senate recognition for it. I am excited about the activities we are planning for next semester, and I look forward to working with you closely as we grow the club.

I also want to thank you, specifically and sincerely, for the many articles of mine you have liked, shared, and promoted throughout the semester. The visibility that created directly led to interview opportunities and invitations to international technical conferences, outcomes I genuinely could not have engineered on my own. I look forward to collaborating with you well beyond this semester, on AI Skunkworks initiatives and on anything else interesting and curious that comes next. Thank you, Professor. It has been a remarkable semester.

With sincere gratitude,

Aravind Balaji
